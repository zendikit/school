    N5 Grammar

    あげる

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語を教えてあげます。
    I will teach you Japanese.
    この本をあげます。
    I'll give you this book.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating that the speaker gives something or some action to someone else. When speaking to someone of lower status, あげるcan be replaced with やる. The honorific form is さしあげる.

    あとで (後で)

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語を勉強した後で寝ました。
    I slept after studying Japanese.
    クラスの後で帰りました。
    I went home after class.
    1. A conjunction indicating that something takes place after something else.

    か

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私は英語か日本語を勉強します。
    I will study either English or Japanese.
    明日授業があるかないか分かりません。
    I don't know whether there is class tomorrow or not.
    1. A grammar particle which indicates an alternative.

    かた (方)

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 その漢字の書き方が分かりません。
    I don't know how to write that kanji.
    1. A nominalizing suffix indicating a way of doing something.

    から

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 来年日本へ行くから日本語を勉強する。
    I'm studying Japanese because I'm going to Japan next year.
    高かったから買いませんでした。
    I didn't buy it because it was expensive.
    2 日本語を勉強してから全部を忘れました。
    After studying Japanese, I forgot everything.
    1. A conjunction indicating a reason or cause.
    2. A conjunction indicating after or since something takes place.

    くらい

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 百五十円くらいでした。
    It was about 150 yen.
    二時間くらいかかります。
    It takes about 2 hours.
    1. A particle indicating an approximate quantity or otherwise translated as "about." ぐらい has the same meaning.

    くれる

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 彼は日本語を教えてくれました。
    He taught me Japanese.
    彼はこの本をくれました。
    He gave me this book.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating that someone gives something or does some action for someone else. The honorific form is くださる.

    こと

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語で書くことは難し。
    It's difficult to write in Japanese.
    1. A nominalizer; it suggests the speaker is not as emotionally close to the concept compared to if the speaker had used の.

    こんな

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 こんな本を買いたいです。
    I'd like to buy a book like this.
    そんなことしないでよ！
    Don't do such a thing!
    1. A word meaning "such a thing" or "this sort of thing." It can be adjusted for proximity to the speaker (そんな、あんな) or be made inquisitive (どんな).

    こんなに

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 そんなに難しかった？
    Was it that difficult?
    こんなに美味しいこと誰でも好きです。
    Anyone likes something delicious like this.
    1. A word meaning "like this." It can be adjusted for proximity to the speaker (そんなに、あんなに).

    さ

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語の難しさがよく分かってる。
    I know the difficulty of Japanese.
    1. A nominalizing suffix attachable to the stem of adjectives.

    しか

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語しか勉強しません。
    I study only Japanese.
    1. A particle indicating that something S and only S is the case, often translated as "nothing," "nobody," or "only."

    すぐ

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 朝起きてすぐに日本語を勉強します。
    As soon as I wake up, I study Japanese.
    学校は駅のすぐ前です。
    The school is right in front of the station.
    1. An adverb indicating not much temporal or physical distance, often translated as "as soon as," "immediately," "right."

    たい

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私は日本語を勉強したいです。
    I want to study Japanese.
    私は刺身を食べたいです。
    I want to eat sashimi.
    1. An auxiliary adjective indicating the desire to do something.

    だけ

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語が勉強しただけです。
    I just studied (and did nothing else with) Japanese.
    私だけ日本語を話せます。
    Only I can speak Japanese.
    1. A particle expressing a limit on something, often translated as "just," "merely," "only," or "that's all."

    だろう

    See でしょう.

    ため

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 試験のために勉強します。
    I'm studying in preparation for exams.
    日本語を勉強するために日本に行きます。
    I'm going to Japan in order to study Japanese.
    病気になったため、クラスに行かなかった。
    Because I got sick, I didn't go to class.
    1. A noun expressing a purpose, reason, or cause.

    って

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 彼女は日本語を勉強するって言った。
    She said she studies Japanese.
    私は日本語を勉強しようかって思います。
    I wonder if I should study Japanese.
    1. A colloquial particle indicating a quotation.

    て

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私は日本へ行って勉強しました。
    I went to Japan and studied.
    これは高くて美味しい。
    This is expensive and delicious.
    彼は先生で優しい。
    He is a teacher, and he is nice.
    1. A verb or i-adjective ending indicating "and." For na-adjectives and nouns, て becomes で instead.

    で

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本人ははしで食べます。
    Japanese people eat with chopsticks.
    これは日本語で何ですか。
    What is this in Japanese?
    1. A grammar particle which indicates the use of something for doing something.

    でしょう

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 彼は日本語で話すでしょう。
    He will probably speak in Japanese.
    この本は高いだろう。
    This book is probably expensive.
    1. An auxiliary indicating conjecture grounded in no substantial evidence. だろう is the informal form.

    でも

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語でも話せます。
    I can even speak Japanese.
    子供でもそのことをできます。
    Even a child can do that.
    1. A particle meaning "even."

    と

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私はあなたと日本語を勉強します。
    I study Japanese with you.
    私は日本人と結婚しました。
    I (got) married (to) a Japanese person.
    1. A grammar particle indicating a reciprocal relationship with the subject.

    どう

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語でどう言いますか。
    How do you say it in Japanese?
    本はどうでしたか。
    How was the book?
    1. A grammar particle which asks about the state of something or someone or the way of doing something.

    と同じくらい〜

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 この本はその本と同じくらい面白い。
    This book is as interesting as that one.
    1. A phrase indicating that one thing is as 〜 as another.

    とき

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語を勉強する時本をたくさん読みます。
    When I study Japanese I read a lot of books.
    静かな時勉強できます。
    I'm able to study when it's quiet.
    1. A dependent noun indicating a time when someone or something did or does something.

    どちら

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語と英語と、どちらが面白い？
    Between Japanese and English, which is more interesting?
    1. A word meaning "which," among a group of things, particularly two.

    どれ

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 黄色と紫色と青色で、どれが一番美しいですか。
    Between yellow, purple, and blue, which is the most beautiful?
    1. A word meaning "which," among a group of 3 or more.

    ないで

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 勉強しないで学校へ来ました。
    I came to school without studying.
    1. A negative te-form indicating not doing something or without doing something.

    なさい

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語を勉強しなさい。
    Study Japanese.
    1. An auxiliary verb which creates an imperative.

    ながら

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 本を読みながら学校へ歩きました。
    I walked to school while reading a book.
    1. A conjunction indicating the surrounding clauses happen simultaneously, often translated as "while" or "with."

    なる

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 あなたの日本語が上手になりました。
    You've become good at Japanese.
    寒くなりました。
    It's become cold.
    1. A word meaning "to become" or "come to be."

    に

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語を勉強（し）に学校へ行きます。
    I'm going to school (in order) to study Japanese.
    本を買いに来ました。
    I came (in order) to buy a book.
    1. A particle indicating the purpose of movement. This construct is only used with motion verbs like 行く, 来る, 帰る, 入る, and 出る.

    の

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 本は日本語のを買いました。
    As for books, I bought a Japanese one.
    あの本はトムのです。
    That book is Tom's book.
    2 どうして勉強しているの？
    Why are you studying (you've got to tell me)?
    明日テストがあるの。
    Because there's a test tomorrow.
    3 勉強するのは難しい。
    Studying is difficult.
    彼が日本語を話すのを聞きました。
    I heard him speaking Japanese.
    1. A dependent, indefinite pronoun indicating "one" or "_'s."
    2. An informal sentence-final particle indicating an explanation or asking for an explanation, used by children or female speakers. If male speakers use it, it is only for questions.
    3. A clause nominalizer.

    のです

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 何をしているんですか。
    What are you doing (you've got to tell me)?
    日本語を勉強しているのです。
    (The explanation is that) I'm studying Japanese.
    1. A sentence-ending phrase indicating an explanation or asking for an explanation. An informal form is んです.

    ばかり

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語ばかり勉強しています。
    I'm studying only Japanese.
    私は勉強したばかりです。
    I studied and haven't done anything else.
    1. A particle indicating that something is the only state, thing, or action.

    ほう

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 あなたの本はどっちですか。
    Which book is yours?
    大きいほうです。
    The bigger one.
    好きなほうを取ってください。
    Please take the one you like most.
    1. A word indicating more than or greater in a comparison. It comes from the form ほうが〜より.

    ほうが〜より

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語のほうが英語より面白い。
    Japanese is more interesting than English.
    遊ぶほうが勉強するより楽しい。
    Playing is more fun than studying.
    1. A phrase for comparing two things, where one is more or greater than the other.

    ほど

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 英語は日本語ほど面白くない。
    English is not as interesting as Japanese.
    1. A particle indicating an extent or degree, often translated as "[not] as ~ as ~."

    まえに (前に)

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 寝る前に日本語を勉強します。
    I study Japanese before I sleep.
    テストの前に勉強します。
    I'll study before the test.
    1. A conjunction indicating in front of or before something occurs.

    まだ

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 まだ日本語を分かりません。
    I still don't understand Japanese.
    私はまだ勉強しています。
    I'm still studying.
    1. An adverb indicating that someone or something is still in a state they were previously in, often translated as "still" or "(not) yet."

    〜ましょう

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語を勉強しましょう。
    Let's study Japanese.
    日本語を勉強しましょうか。
    Shall we study Japanese?
    1. A formal volitional verb ending indicating the speaker's intent or invitation to do something.

    〜ませんか

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語を勉強しませんか。
    Would you like to study Japanese?
    2 一緒に勉強しない？
    Wanna study together?
    1. The formal negative question form of a verb can be used to extend an invitation formally.
    2. The informal non-past negative form of a verb can be used to extend an invitation informally.

    まで

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私は五時まで日本語を勉強します。
    I will study Japanese until 5 o'clock.
    東京まで3時間かかります。
    It takes 3 hours until Tokyo.
    1. A grammar particle which indicates a spacial or temporal limit.

    もう

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 もう日本語を勉強しましたか。
    Have you already studied Japanese?
    もう本がありません。
    I don't have the book anymore.
    1. And adverb indicating that someone or something is no longer in a state they were previously in, often translated as "already" or "(not) any longer."

    もらう

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 友達はこの本を買ってもらいました。
    My friend bought me this book.
    友達にこの本をもらいました。
    I received this book from my friend.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating that the speaker or someone else receives something or some benefit from an action from someone else. The honorific form is いただく.

    よう

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語を勉強しよう。
    Let's study Japanese.
    地球を守ろう。
    Let's protect the Earth.
    1. An informal volition verb ending indicating the speaker's intent or invitation to do something.

    ようとおもう (ようと思う)

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語を勉強しようと思います。
    I think I'll study Japanese.
    英語で話すまいと思います。
    I think I won't speak in English.
    1. A phrase indicating that the speaker desires to do something. The negative form is まいと思う.

    られる

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 漢字を読める。
    I can read kanji.
    刺身を食べられます。
    I can eat sashimi.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating the potential to do something.

    を

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私は日本語を勉強します。
    I study Japanese.
    2 私は公園を歩きました。
    I walked through the park.
    1. A grammar particle indicating a direct object.
    2. A grammar particle indicating a space through or along which someone or something moves.
