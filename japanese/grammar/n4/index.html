    N4 Grammar

    いたす

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私がいたします。
    I will do it.
    1. A humble (謙譲語) form of する used when talking about yourself.

    おわる (終わる)

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私はこの本を読み終わりました。
    I finished reading this book.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating that someone or something finishes doing something.

    お〜になる

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 先生はレポートをもうお読みになりました。
    The teacher has already read the report.
    これは、先生がお書きになった手紙です。
    This is a letter the teacher wrote.
    1. A phrase used to form honorific statements about someone's actions or state.

    か

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私は本を読むかテレビを見るかどちらかです。
    I will either read a book or watch TV.
    私は本か漫画を読みます。
    I will read either a book or manga.
    1. A particle indicating an alternative.

    が

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 クラスには本があります。
    There is a book in the classroom.
    1. A particle which indicates the subject of a clause.

    かどうか

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 漢字を覚えるかどうか分からない。
    I don't know whether or not I can learn kanji.
    高かったかどうか知りません。
    I don't know whether or not it was expensive.
    1. A particle meaning "whether or not;" the phrase preceding it must be a yes/no question.

    ことがある

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私は日本語を勉強したことある。
    I have studied Japanese.
    私は日本語を話せなかったことがある。
    There was a time when I couldn't speak Japanese.
    1. A phrase indicating that there was a time when something happened.

    させてください

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 ローマ字で書かせてください。
    Can I write in romaji?
    これを食べさせてください。
    Can I eat this?
    1. An auxiliary verb used to ask for permission to do something.

    させられる

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私は先生に漢字で書かせられた。
    I was made to write in kanji by the teacher.
    友達は彼女に刺身を食べさせられた。
    My friend was made to eat sashimi by his girlfriend.
    1. An auxiliary verb meaning to be made to do something.

    させる

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 先生は私に漢字を書かせた。
    The teacher made me write kanji.
    私は友達に刺身を食べさせた。
    I made my friend eat sashimi.
    1. An auxiliary verb meaning to cause someone or something to do something.

    すぎる

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 この本は難しすぎる。
    This book is too difficult.
    私は勉強しすぎた。
    I studied too much.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating that something is excessive.

    たら

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 もしテストがあったら勉強する。
    If there is a test, then I'll study.
    高かったら買わない。
    If it's expensive, then I won't buy it.
    1. A conjunction used to express "if a then b" or "when a then b."

    たり

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語は難しかったり易しかったりです。
    Japanese is sometimes difficult and sometimes easy.
    学生は読んだり勉強したりです。
    Students do things like reading and writing.
    1. A phrase indicating a list of things non-exhaustively.

    だす (出す)

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 先生は急に話し出した。
    The teacher suddenly started speaking.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating that something has started.

    てある

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私はもう勉強してある。
    I've already studied [perhaps for some upcoming exam].
    説明はこの本には書いてあります。
    The explanation is written in this book.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating that something is in a resultant state, typically in anticipation or preparation for something else.

    ていく

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 これから難しくなっていきます。
    It will get more difficult from here on.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating a continuously changing state, often translated as "to go on ~ing."

    ておく

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本に行くから日本語を勉強しておきます。
    I'll study Japanese in advance because I'm going to Japan.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating that someone does something in advance for something or someone else.

    てくださいませんか

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語を教えてくださいませんか。
    Would you please teach me Japanese?
    3 時に来てくださいませんか。
    Would you please come at 3?
    1. An auxiliary verb used to politely make a request.

    てしまう

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 この本を読んでしまった。
    I finished reading this book.
    2 友達のビールを飲んでしまいました。
    I accidentally drank my friend's beer.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating completion of an action.
    2. An auxiliary verb indicating that someone did something they should not have or that something happened that should not have.

    てみる

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 この漢字を読んでみてください。
    Please try to read this kanji.
    1. An auxiliary verb meaning to try something.

    てもいい

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 この本を読んでもいいですか。
    Is it alright if I read this book?
    難しくてもいいです。
    It's alright if it's difficult.
    日本人じゃなくてもいいです。
    It's alright if you're not Japanese.
    1. A phrase expressing permission. A more polite form replaces いい with かまい ません, and an even more polite form replaces いい with よろしい.

    で

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 この本を百円で買いました。
    I bought this book for 100 yen.
    学校は 3 時で終わります。
    School ends at 3 o'clock.
    私は一時間でテストをしました。
    I completed the test in one hour.
    1. A particle indicating how much time something takes, money something costs, or when something ends.

    つづける （続ける）

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 本を読み続けてください。
    Please continue reading the book.
    1. An auxiliary verb meaning, "to keep on doing" or "to continue."

    と

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 先生に聞くと分かる。
    If you ask the teacher, you'll understand.
    テストは難しくないと誰でも合格できます。
    If the test is not difficult, anyone can pass it.
    1. A conjunction indicating a conditional with an uncontrollable consequence.

    という

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 試験は難しかったという聞いた。
    I heard that the test was hard.
    「げんき」という教科書を使っている。
    I'm using a textbook called "Genki."
    1. A phrase marking information describing the thing that follows, often translated as "that" or "is called."

    とか

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私は勉強するとか読むが好きです。
    I like things such as studying and reading.
    私は映画とかアニメとかは見ます。
    I watch things such as movies and anime.
    1. A conjunction listing two or more things as examples, inexhaustively.

    なさる

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 何なさいますか。
    What did you do?
    先生はもうお散歩なさいました。
    The teacher already went for a walk.
    1. The honorific (尊敬語) form of する.

    なければならない

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 漢字を勉強しなければならない。
    You must study kanji.
    図書館で静かでなくてはいけない。
    You must be quiet in the library.
    割引は学生でないといけない。
    For the discount, you must be a student.
    1. A phrase indicating that someone must do something or that something must be in some state. There are numerous variations of this phrase.

    なら

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 難しいなら勉強しません。
    If it's difficult, I won't study it.
    日本語が好きなら勉強した方がいい。
    If you like Japanese, you should study it.
    1. A conjunction meaning "if."

    にくい

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 この本は読みにくいです。
    This book is hard to read.
    1. An auxiliary adjective indicating that someone or something is difficult to ~.

    ば

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 高ければ買いません。
    If it's expensive, I won't buy it.
    カフェは静かならば勉強できる。
    If the cafe is quiet, I can study.
    公園に行けば帽子をかぶった方がいい。
    If you go to the park, you should wear a hat.
    1. A conjunction meaning "if."

    はいけない

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 授業で話してはいけません。
    You must not talk in class.
    1. A phrase indicating prohibition.

    はじめる (始める)

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私はこの本を読み始めました。
    I started to read this book.
    1. An auxiliary verb indicating that someone or something starts something else.

    ほうがいい (方がいい)

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 漢字を勉強した方がいい。
    You should study kanji.
    勉強をスキップしない方がいいです。
    You should not skip studying.
    1. A phrase indicating that it is strongly suggested that someone do something.

    も

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私はこの本も読めます。
    I can read even this book.
    私はその本を見もしてない。
    I haven't even seen that book.
    2 私は日本語も話します。
    I speak Japanese, too.
    私も日本語を話します。
    I, too, speak Japanese.
    1. A particle indicating emphasis of some object, quantity, concept, etc.
    2. A particle often translated as "also" or "too."

    や

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 日本語の本や英語の本を読みます。
    I read Japanese and English books, among others.
    1. A conjunction listing two or more nouns as examples, inexhaustively.

    やすい

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 この本は読みやすいです。
    This book is easy to read.
    1. An auxiliary adjective indicating that someone or something is easy to ~.

    られる

    Grammar Point Sentence
    1 私は先生に叱られた。
    I was scolded by the teacher.
    私は先生から聞かれた。
    I was asked by the teacher.
    2 私のレポートをもう読まれましたか。
    Have you already read my report?
    これは、先生が書かれた手紙です。
    This is a letter the teacher wrote.
    1. An auxiliary verb used to construct passive statements.
    2. An auxiliary verb used to form honorifics with a politeness less than ますに なる.
